# Review Patterns

Use these patterns when reviewing learner work.

## 1. Verdict Format

Prefer this top-level structure:

- Verdict: pass, mostly correct, not correct yet, or wrong direction
- Findings: ordered by severity
- Next action: one concrete next step

## 2. Findings Style

Each finding should include:

1. what is wrong or missing
2. why it matters for this lesson
3. what would prove it is fixed

## 3. Severity Guide

### High

The learner misunderstood the main concept or produced behavior that contradicts the lesson goal.

### Medium

The learner is on the right path but skipped a required step, verification, or explanation.

### Low

The answer is acceptable but weak, ambiguous, or missing a useful production note.

## 4. When To Withhold Full Solutions

Prefer hints or targeted corrections when:

- the learner has not shown a real attempt yet
- the problem can be fixed with one or two nudges
- the goal is conceptual understanding rather than shipping code

Give a full worked answer when:

- the learner explicitly asks for it
- repeated attempts show they are stuck
- the lesson value now comes from understanding the explanation of the solution

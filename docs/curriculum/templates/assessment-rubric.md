# Assessment Rubric Template

| Criterion | Insufficient | Meets expected level | Strong evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Correctness | Does not reach the stated outcome. | Outcome is verified by the stated check. | Verifies assumptions and relevant edge cases. |
| Safety | Risks secrets, data, or uncontrolled resources. | Uses safe defaults and cleanup. | Identifies residual risk and a rollback boundary. |
| Reasoning | Gives commands without an explanation. | Explains the chosen approach and constraints. | Compares alternatives and records a defensible tradeoff. |
| Operability | Cannot be monitored or recovered. | Includes validation and recovery information. | Includes signals, runbook steps, and follow-up improvements. |
| Communication | Evidence is incomplete or unclear. | Provides required artifacts and concise rationale. | Communicates status, impact, and prioritization for others. |

For a capstone or game day, reviewers score each criterion independently and record evidence references. A passing score must not be based solely on a final healthy response.

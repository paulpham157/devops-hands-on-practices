# Learner Personas and Level Boundaries

These personas guide routing and assessment scope. They are not labels that determine a learner's worth or job title; use demonstrated evidence to place someone at a level.

| Persona | Starting context | Primary need | Recommended route | Evidence focus |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Foundation learner | Terminal familiarity, little operations experience | Build safe mental models and local confidence | Level 1 | Guided labs, explanations, cleanup. |
| Delivery practitioner | Can code or operate services, limited delivery ownership | Assemble repeatable source-to-deployment flow | Level 2 shared core | IaC, CI/security, deployment, secrets, telemetry, reference project. |
| Production operator | Has seen incidents or owns a service | Improve diagnosis, mitigation, recovery, and tradeoffs | Level 3 preparation | Game days, SLO decisions, capacity/cost, DR, migration. |
| Platform/SRE specialist | Works on internal platforms or reliability | Turn operational patterns into a supported platform/product | Level 3 + overlay | Golden path, platform metrics, error budget, adoption and escape hatches. |
| Senior-capstone candidate | Has real production ownership and a portfolio | Defend cross-domain decisions under constraints | Level 4 preparation | ADRs, threat model, DR, migration, game day, design defense. |
| Application/data specialist | Strong application or data background, needs operations impact | Connect architecture/data choices to reliability | Level 1 diagnostic + relevant overlay | Protocol/data ownership, quality, SLO and recovery tradeoffs. |

## Routing Rules

1. A target job title never skips an entry check.
2. A learner may skip a level only with equivalent evidence and a reviewer decision.
3. Tool familiarity is not equivalent to production ownership.
4. Learners can combine a role overlay with the shared core, but an overlay cannot replace the shared core.
5. Cloud accounts and paid services are optional for core evidence.

# Design Defense Protocol

## Before the Defense

1. Learner submits required artifacts and a one-page executive summary.
2. Two reviewers score independently using `rubric.md`.
3. Reviewers prepare questions about assumptions, alternatives, failure handling, and evidence gaps; they do not prepare a preferred solution.

## Defense Format

- 10 minutes: learner explains the system, constraints, and highest-risk decision.
- 20 minutes: reviewers test tradeoffs, incident response, migration/rollback, and cost/reliability reasoning.
- 10 minutes: learner identifies what they would validate next and what they would defer.
- 10 minutes: reviewers record scores, evidence, disagreements, and follow-up actions.

The defense may be live or written/asynchronous. A learner may revise artifacts after feedback; the revision record must state what changed and why.

## Reviewer Guardrails

- Judge evidence and reasoning, not familiarity with a reviewer’s preferred cloud/vendor/tool.
- Do not require real credentials, customer data, or paid infrastructure.
- Treat a clearly bounded uncertainty with a validation plan as better than unsupported confidence.
- Do not infer job seniority from a capstone pass.

# Capstone Review Rubric

Two reviewers independently score each criterion before discussing differences. Cite the submitted artifact/evidence for every score.

| Criterion | Does not yet meet | Meets capstone expectation | Strong production judgment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Architecture and tradeoffs | Diagram or decisions ignore constraints. | Design addresses traffic, team, budget, and failure constraints. | Alternatives, reversibility, and consequences are explicit and coherent. |
| Security and identity | Trust boundaries or secret handling are absent. | Identity, authorization, and secret lifecycle are specified with residual risk. | Threat model drives practical controls and exception handling. |
| Delivery and migration | Happy-path deployment only. | Promotion, rollback, compatibility, and migration checks are defined. | Safely balances delivery speed with measured risk and recovery. |
| Observability and reliability | Metrics or SLOs are detached from user journeys. | Signals, objectives, alerts, and recovery decisions are connected. | Uses error budget and measurement limits to prioritize work. |
| Data, DR, capacity, and cost | Assumptions are hidden or impossible to validate. | RPO/RTO, capacity, cost drivers, and validation plan are explicit. | Chooses investments based on risk, constraints, and evidence. |
| Incident ownership | Only restores a healthy response. | Shows timeline, communication, mitigation, recovery, and blameless follow-up. | Identifies systemic improvements and ownership without blame. |
| Communication and leadership | Artifacts are difficult to review or decisions are unexplained. | Clear, concise, evidence-linked design defense. | Anticipates stakeholder concerns and prioritizes feasible improvements. |

Passing guideline: no criterion may be “Does not yet meet”; at least five criteria must meet expectation; reviewers must resolve material score differences in writing. A pass shows curriculum-level production judgment, not workplace seniority.
